Web site Critiques

TRADER JOE'S

https://www.traderjoes.com/

Questions

  1. What was your first impression of the site?
  2. How does this site organize the flow of information? (Do they have links on the left and text on the right? Some other scheme? No scheme?)
  3. Does the site structure make sense or does it confuse you? Why?
  4. Does the site graphics/layout/multimedia enchance the site or detract from it? Why?
  5. How long does it take for the home page to load? Was the laod time reasonable?
  6. Was the text color and size readable? If a background color or image was used, did it enhance or detract from the site?
  7. Did the site have a navigation bar or other navigation tool? Was the navigation well done?
  8. Did the site have a color scheme or look and feel that was repeated on all the pages?
  9. How does the site respond to smaller window sizes (responsive design)?
  10. What does this site offer that was unusual or very well done?

My first impression of the site wasn't the best. I am a big Trader Joe's fan--I love their style and vibe. I feel like the vibe of the webiste is a bit bare, with less focus on the cool stuff they have and more on recipes.

The site's organization matches their wesbite's vibe: minimalistic. It There are only 5 drop down links, with most of them being things divorced from the store itself.

I think the site's structure confuses me a little bit as I feel the focus is on recipes rather than products or promotions (which is usually the purpose of a grocery store)

The site graphics/layout/multimedia is the saving grace of this site. I've been very negative about my review, but I really do feel like Trader Joe's vibe comes through in its design.

The load time was reasonable, maybe 3 seconds.

The text color was good! The background color was just a bland white, which I felt took away from the overall design.

The site has a small navigation bar and it is pretty well done. Just a little bit random.

The website's color scheme was consistent but nothing out of the ordinary. Just white and red. Definitely repeated throughout the website.

There is definitely a responsive design in place with the wesbite. When I resize the window, the products stay lines up and some lesser important things are hidden or moved to a different layout.

The site has a few unusual features. I thought the focus on recipes and podcast was a different direction from what is expected from a grocery store. Overall it is a very minimalistic site
